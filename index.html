Tribute to The Late HON.Mwai Kibaki

Third President of Kenya (1931 - 2022)

The late HON. Mwai Kibaki served as the third President of Kenya from 2002 to 2013. Born on November 15, 1931, in Gatuyaini village, Nyeri County, he was a distinguished economist, politician, and statesman who transformed Kenya's political and economic landscape.

The late HON. Mwai Kibaki is remembered for his strong leadership, economic reforms, and the landmark introduction of free primary education program in 2003, which increased enrollment from 5.9 million to over 7 million children. His administration oversaw significant economic growth, with Kenya's GDP growing at an average of 4-7% annually during his leadership.

A Cambridge University graduate with a degree in Economics, Public Finance, and Political Science, HON. Mwai Kibaki served in various ministerial positions under Presidents Jomo Kenyatta and Daniel arap Moi before ascending to the presidency. He was instrumental in Kenya's transition to multiparty democracy and is credited with restoring economic stability, fighting corruption, and promoting national unity after the 2007-2008 post-election violence.

Major Achievements

  • Introduced Free Primary Education in 2003.
  • Improved Kenya's economy with strong growth during his leadership.
  • Expanded road networks and infrastructure.
  • Encouraged democratic reforms and governance.
